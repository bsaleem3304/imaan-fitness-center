Knowing Our Creator

Understand, reflect, and connect with Allah through His beautiful and majestic names — Asma ul-Husna.

The Prophet ﷺ said: "Allah has ninety-nine Names. Whoever enumerates them will enter Paradise." — Bukhari & Muslim

✦ Scholarly Perspective: Understanding the 99 Names of Allah

Classical Islamic scholars have long discussed an important clarification regarding this blessed hadith. While the Messenger of Allah ﷺ mentioned "ninety-nine Names," Islamic scholarship has established that Allah's Names and Attributes are not limited to only ninety-nine. Rather, the ninety-nine Names refer to the complete set of Divine Names that have been explicitly preserved and transmitted to us through the Quran and the authentic Sunnah.

The renowned scholar Ibn Qayyim al-Jawziyyah explained that Allah's Names are not restricted to a specific number. He stated that the Lord Almighty possesses infinite perfection, and His Names and Attributes extend beyond enumeration. The Prophet ﷺ specifically enumerated ninety-nine Names as a guidance for the believers — these are the Names that have been preserved in their explicit form within our Islamic sources and are the ones through which the believer may call upon Allah.

Similarly, Imam al-Qurtubi and other classical scholars noted that the Quran itself contains Names and Attributes of Allah beyond the enumerated ninety-nine. For instance, the Quran describes Allah with attributes such as "al-Muhyi" (The Giver of Life), "al-Mumit" (The Taker of Life), and others. The phrase "Whoever enumerates them" in the hadith means whoever knows and memorizes the preserved ninety-nine Names with understanding, implementing their meanings in heart and deed, shall enter Paradise.

Contemporary scholars continue this understanding. Shaykh Muhammad al-Salih al-Uthaymeen explained that the virtue and benefit lie in deeply understanding and applying even these ninety-nine Names in one's life, recognizing Allah through them, and calling upon Him through them. Whether Allah possesses additional Names beyond these is a matter known fully only to Allah; what matters is that we engage profoundly with the Names He has made known to us.

This understanding harmonizes beautifully with the principle in Islamic jurisprudence: while we affirm the infinitude of Allah's perfection and attributes, we practically focus our devotion, supplication, and knowledge on those Names and Attributes that Allah Himself has clarified for us. This approach balances our acknowledgment of Allah's infinite perfection with the practical guidance the Prophet ﷺ has left for humanity.

✦ Why Knowing Allah's Names and Attributes is Essential

To know our Creator, one must reflect upon the signs that exist all around and within oneself. The universe operates in perfect order and complete obedience to its Creator. The sun rises and sets by His command, the earth rotates according to His law, and every creation follows the path ordained for it. Likewise, within the human being, the heart beats without permission, the breath flows without effort, and every cell submits to the will of its Lord. These signs make it clear that everything in existence is already in submission to the Creator.

When a person learns the Names and Attributes of Allah ﷻ — His mercy, His power, His wisdom, and His authority — the heart begins to recognize the One it was always obeying. This recognition transforms submission into a conscious act filled with certainty, humility, and love. In knowing Allah ﷻ through His perfect Attributes, the soul finds peace, the heart finds purpose, and submission becomes the natural response to the Creator of all things.

Knowing Allah through His Names and Attributes is the greatest path to strengthening īmān, purifying the heart, and establishing true tawakkul. A believer cannot truly love Allah, fear Him properly, rely upon Him sincerely, or worship Him completely unless they know who Allah is.

وَمَا خَلَقْتُ الْجِنَّ وَالْإِنسَ إِلَّا لِيَعْبُدُونِ
"I did not create jinn and mankind except to worship Me."
Surah adh-Dhāriyāt 51:56

When a believer knows Allah is Ar-Rahmān, their heart fills with hope. When they know Allah is Al-Qadīr, they stop fearing creation. When they know Allah is Ar-Razzāq, they stop relying on wealth and employers as their source of provision. They still use the means — but their heart relies only on Allah, the Creator of the means.

وَلِلَّهِ ٱلْأَسْمَاءُ ٱلْحُسْنَىٰ فَٱدْعُوهُ بِهَا
"And to Allah belong the Most Beautiful Names, so call upon Him by them."
Surah al-A'rāf 7:180
إِنَّ لِلَّهِ تِسْعَةً وَتِسْعِينَ اسْمًا، مِائَةً إِلَّا وَاحِدًا، مَنْ أَحْصَاهَا دَخَلَ الْجَنَّةَ
"Allah has ninety-nine Names, one hundred minus one. Whoever enumerates them will enter Paradise."
Sahih Bukhari 2736 · Sahih Muslim 2677

The scholars explained that "enumerating them" includes: memorizing them, understanding them, calling upon Allah using them, and living according to their meanings. This transforms the believer from someone dependent on creation to someone fully dependent on the Creator.

In-Depth Study · Name 1 of 99

Allah

The greatest and most comprehensive of all the Names — the foundation of tawḥīd itself.

اللَّهُ
Allah — The One True God, The Only One Worthy of Worship

✦Introduction to This Name

The Name "Allah" is the greatest and most comprehensive of all the Names of Allah. It is the Proper Name that encompasses every other Beautiful Name and Perfect Attribute. Every Name returns to it. Every act of worship is directed to it.

When a believer says "Allah," they are calling upon the One who is:

Ar-Rahmān (The Entirely Merciful) · Al-Malik (The King) · Al-'Azīz (The All-Mighty) · Al-'Alīm (The All-Knowing) · and every other Perfect Name.

This Name is the foundation of tawḥīd (absolute oneness of Allah). Without understanding this Name, faith (īmān) remains incomplete.

1Name in Arabic

اللَّهُ

2Transliteration

Allah

3English Meaning

The One True God · The Only One worthy of worship · The One who possesses all Perfect Names and Attributes.

Unlike other Names, "Allah" is not derived from an attribute. It is the proper Name of the Divine Being Himself.

4Use of This Name in the Qur'an

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ
"Allah — there is no deity worthy of worship except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer."
Surah al-Baqarah 2:255 — Ayat al-Kursi

This verse establishes both His Name and His exclusive right to worship.

قُلْ هُوَ اللَّهُ أَحَدٌ
"Say: He is Allah, One."
Surah al-Ikhlāṣ 112:1

This affirms His absolute oneness (tawḥīd).

اللَّهُ خَالِقُ كُلِّ شَيْءٍ
"Allah is the Creator of all things."
Surah az-Zumar 39:62

This establishes His Lordship (rubūbiyyah — exclusive control and ownership).

5Use of This Name in Hadith

"The best statement ever said by me and the Prophets before me is: There is no deity worthy of worship except Allah."

"Whoever says 'La ilaha illa Allah' sincerely will enter Paradise."
Sahih al-Bukhari

6Root Word Analysis

Scholars differed regarding the linguistic origin of "Allah." Many linguists said it comes from Ilāh (deity or object of worship) — something that is worshipped, loved, feared, and relied upon.

When prefixed with "Al" (The): Al-Ilāh → Allah — The One and Only True Deity deserving worship.

"This Name gathers all meanings of the Beautiful Names."
Ibn al-Qayyim

Unlike other Names, this Name refers to the Divine Essence (dhāt — the Being of Allah), not only to an attribute.

الرَّبّ
Ar-Rabb — The Lord, The Master, The Sustainer, The Nurturer

Introduction to This Name

Among the Names of Allah, the Name Ar-Rabb is one of the most frequently mentioned in the Qur'an. Nearly every supplication (du'ā) of the Prophets begins with:

رَبَّنَا
"Our Lord…"

This Name captures the intimate relationship between the servant and Allah. While the Name Allah establishes who is worshipped, and Ar-Raḥmān establishes divine mercy, the Name Ar-Rabb establishes Lordship — absolute ownership, authority, and nurturing control. Understanding Ar-Rabb reshapes how a believer sees hardship, provision, guidance, growth, and destiny.

Name in Arabic & Transliteration

الرَّبّ
Arabic
Ar-Rabb
Transliteration

English meanings:

  • The Lord
  • The Master
  • The Owner
  • The Sustainer
  • The Nurturer
  • The One who develops His creation gradually and perfectly

The Arabic word "Rabb" is far richer than the English word "Lord." It combines multiple meanings simultaneously.

The Four Essential Dimensions of Ar-Rabb

As explained by scholars based on classical linguistic sources, the word Rabb carries four essential qualities:

١
Owner — Mālik (Complete Possessor)

A Rabb is one who owns something entirely. Everything belongs to Him — your body, your wealth, your time, your family, your abilities, your future. Nothing belongs to you independently.

الْحَمْدُ لِلَّهِ رَبِّ الْعَالَمِينَ
"All praise belongs to Allah, Lord of the worlds."
Surah al-Fātiḥah 1:2
Effect on the Heart: Removes arrogance and entitlement. Everything becomes an amānah (trust from Allah).
٢
Master — Sayyid (Authority Who Must Be Obeyed)

A Rabb is not only Owner — He is Master. Ownership without authority is incomplete. Allah commands, and His command must be obeyed. This connects directly to:

إِيَّاكَ نَعْبُدُ
"You alone we worship."
Surah al-Fātiḥah 1:5
Effect on the Heart: Submission increases. You obey not because you agree emotionally, but because He is Rabb.
٣
Sustainer — Qayyim (Constant Provider and Maintainer)

A Rabb continuously maintains what He owns. Allah does not create and abandon. He sustains every breath, every heartbeat, every atom.

اللَّهُ لَا إِلَٰهَ إِلَّا هُوَ الْحَيُّ الْقَيُّومُ
"Allah — there is no deity worthy of worship except Him, the Ever-Living, the Sustainer."
Surah al-Baqarah 2:255
Effect on the Heart: Tawakkul (reliance upon Allah) strengthens. Provision is not from your employer — it is from your Rabb through your employer.
٤
Nurturer — Murabbī (One Who Develops Gradually)

The root ر ب ب carries the meaning of gradual development. Just as a gardener nurtures a plant step by step, Allah nurtures your faith (īmān), your character, your trials, and your life path. Hardships are not random events — they are spiritual cultivation.

Effect on the Heart: Sabr (patience) increases. You begin to see difficulty as development. You trust the process written by your Rabb.

Use of This Name in the Qur'an

The Prophets consistently invoked Allah as Rabb — reflecting dependence and closeness:

The du'ā of Adam ﷺ
رَبَّنَا ظَلَمْنَا أَنفُسَنَا
"Our Lord, we have wronged ourselves…"
Surah al-A'rāf 7:23
The du'ā of Ibrāhīm ﷺ
رَبِّ اجْعَلْ هَٰذَا بَلَدًا آمِنًا
"My Lord, make this a secure city…"
Surah al-Baqarah 2:126
The du'ā of Mūsā ﷺ
رَبِّ اشْرَحْ لِي صَدْرِي
"My Lord, expand for me my chest."
Surah Ṭā-Hā 20:25

Root Word Analysis

Root: ر ب ب

Classical linguists such as Ibn Fāris explained that this root includes: ownership, rectification, regulation, and gradual development.

"Rabb combines creation, ownership, command, and nurturing."

— Ibn al-Qayyim

Ibn Taymiyyah

Tawḥīd ar-Rubūbiyyah (Oneness of Lordship — believing Allah alone creates, sustains, and controls) is embedded in this Name. Even the disbelievers of Makkah affirmed Allah as Rabb — but they committed shirk in worship. Thus, believing Allah is Rabb must lead to worshipping Him alone.

Imam at-Tabarī

"Rabb is the One who rectifies the affairs of His creation."

الْحَلِيمُ
Al-Ḥalīm — The Forbearing, The Most Patient with His Servants

Introduction to This Name

Among the most comforting Names of Allah is Al-Ḥalīm. Ḥilm (forbearance) means restraining anger despite having the power to punish. It is not weakness — it is controlled strength.

When a human delays punishment, it is often because they are unable to act. But when Allah delays, it is not inability — it is mercy. He gives time for repentance, space for reflection, and allows hearts to return. Every day we wake up is proof of Allah's ḥilm.

الْغَفُورُ
Al-Ghafūr — The Extremely Forgiving

Introduction to This Name

While Al-Ghaffār emphasizes repeated forgiveness, Al-Ghafūr emphasizes depth and magnitude of forgiveness. Allah not only forgives repeatedly — He forgives greatly.

He forgives major sins (kabāʾir — grave sins), repeated sins, and hidden sins. Forgiveness includes covering the sin, removing punishment, and replacing it with reward. This Name prevents despair (qunūṭ — hopelessness in Allah's mercy).

  • The One whose forgiveness includes covering sin, removing punishment, and replacing it with reward
